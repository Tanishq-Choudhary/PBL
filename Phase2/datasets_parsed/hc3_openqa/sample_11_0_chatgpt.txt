what does the president of the usa do

The President of the United States is the head of the executive branch of the federal government and serves as the commander-in-chief of the military. The president is responsible for enforcing the laws of the United States and has the authority to negotiate treaties and make appointments to the federal government, including the Supreme Court. The president is also responsible for creating and implementing the country's foreign and domestic policies. The president works closely with Congress to pass legislation and plays a significant role in shaping the direction of the country. The president is also responsible for representing the country on the international stage and serving as a symbol of national unity.
