what classes are considered humanities

Humanities are academic disciplines that study the human condition, using methods that are primarily analytical, critical, or speculative, as distinguished from the mainly empirical approaches of the natural sciences. The humanities include ancient and modern languages, literature, philosophy, history, archaeology, anthropology, human geography, law, politics, religion, and art.\n\nSome common examples of classes that are considered to be in the humanities include:\n\nArt history\nClassics (e.g., ancient Greek and Roman culture, language, and literature)\nCommunication\nEnglish (e.g., literature, writing, linguistics)\nHistory\nLanguages (e.g., Spanish, French, German, Chinese)\nLinguistics\nMusic\nPhilosophy\nReligious studies\nTheatre\nIt is worth noting that the boundaries between the humanities and other academic disciplines are not always clear, and there is some overlap with fields such as sociology, psychology, and anthropology.
