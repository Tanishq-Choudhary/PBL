what are the characteristics of an ethnic group

An ethnic group is a group of people who share a common culture, language, history, and ancestry. Some characteristics of an ethnic group include:\n\nCommon ancestry: Ethnic groups are often defined by a common ancestry, which may be based on shared genetic characteristics, cultural traditions, or a shared history.\n\nCulture: Ethnic groups often have their own distinct culture, including language, customs, traditions, and religious practices.\n\nLanguage: Many ethnic groups have their own language or dialect, which is used within the group and passed down from generation to generation.\n\nGeographical concentration: Ethnic groups may be concentrated in certain geographical areas, either within a country or across international borders.\n\nSocial organization: Ethnic groups may have their own social and political organizations, such as community organizations or political parties.\n\nShared experiences: Ethnic groups often share common experiences and challenges, such as discrimination or oppression, which can shape their identity and sense of community.
