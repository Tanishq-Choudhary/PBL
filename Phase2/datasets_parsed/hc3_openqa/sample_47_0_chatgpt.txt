what is the function of the liver

The liver is a vital organ located in the upper right side of the abdomen. It has a number of important functions, including:\n\nDetoxification: The liver helps to filter harmful substances from the blood, such as drugs, alcohol, and waste products.\n\nDigestion: The liver produces bile, a substance that helps to digest fats and absorb fat-soluble vitamins.\n\nMetabolism: The liver plays a key role in the body's metabolism, helping to convert stored glycogen into glucose and breaking down fats and proteins.\n\nStorage: The liver stores important nutrients, such as iron and vitamins, as well as excess glucose in the form of glycogen.\n\nBlood clotting: The liver produces proteins that are involved in the blood clotting process.\n\nOverall, the liver is an essential organ that performs a wide range of important functions in the body.
