what food is in afghan

Afghanistan has a rich and varied culinary tradition that is influenced by its history and its geographic location at the crossroads of Central and South Asia. Some common foods in Afghanistan include:\n\nKabuli palaw: a rice dish made with steamed basmati rice, lamb or chicken, and a variety of spices, including cumin, coriander, and cardamom.\n\nQabili palaw: a rice dish made with raisins, carrots, and lamb or chicken, and flavored with a variety of spices, including cumin, coriander, and cardamom.\n\nAshak: a type of dumpling filled with leeks, scallions, and ground meat, and served with a spicy tomato-based sauce.\n\nMantu: a type of dumpling filled with ground meat and onions, and served with a yogurt-based sauce.\n\nKofta: meatballs made with ground lamb or beef, mixed with spices and herbs, and often served with a tomato-based sauce.\n\nKabuli chana: a dish made with chickpeas, tomatoes, onions, and a variety of spices, including cumin, coriander, and turmeric.\n\nNaan: a type of flatbread made with flour, water, and yogurt, and often cooked in a tandoor oven.\n\nChai: a type of tea made with black tea, milk, and spices, including cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves.\n\nQeema: a dish made with minced meat, onions, and a variety of spices, including cumin, coriander, and turmeric.\n\nBaklava: a sweet pastry made with layers of filo dough, filled with nuts and sweetened with honey or syrup.
