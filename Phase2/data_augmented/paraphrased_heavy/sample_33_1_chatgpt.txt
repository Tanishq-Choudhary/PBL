how does a dim sum restaurant work In a dim sum restaurant, customers typically sit at large tables and are served little plates of food called "dim sum" by servers pushing carts around the restaurant.  Some may additionally offer a selection of desserts and beverages.  Dim sum is a type of Cantonese cuisine that consists of a variety of minute dishes, including dumplings, buns, and other bite-sized treats. \n\nIn addition to dim sum, many dim sum restaurants additionally offer a selection of other dishes, such as soups, noodles, and rice dishes.  Dim sum restaurants often operate on a "point system," where customers are charged based on the number of dishes they order.  Alternatively, some dim sum restaurants may also have a menu that customers can order from. \n\nTo order dim sum, customers simply need to flag down a server pushing a cart and form their selection from the dishes on the cart. \n\nThe server will then mark the order on a tally sheet and bring the dishes to the table when they are ready. \n\nOverall, the experience of dining at a dim sum restaurant is typically lively and social, with customers able to sample a variety of different dishes and flavors.
