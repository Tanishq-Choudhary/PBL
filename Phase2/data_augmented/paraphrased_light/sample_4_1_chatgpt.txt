what classes are considered humanities The humanities are a broad academic field that encompasses a range of subjects that deal with human culture, society, and communication. Some common examples of classes that are considered to be in the humanities include:\n\nLiterature: This includes the study of literature in various languages and time periods. This can include poetry, plays, novels, and other forms of written expression.\n\nHistory: This includes the study of the past, including events, people, and cultures.\n\nPhilosophy: This includes the study of abstract concepts and ideas, such as the nature of reality, ethics, and the meaning of life.\n\nArt: This includes the study of visual arts, such as painting, sculpture, and architecture, as well as music, theater, and other performing arts.\n\nAnthropology: This includes the study of human culture, society, and behavior, often through the examination of ancient and contemporary cultures.\n\nReligious studies: This includes the study of religion and spirituality from a variety of perspectives, including the history, beliefs, and practices of different religions.\n\nLinguistics: This includes the study of language and how it is used and acquired.\n\nCommunication: This includes the study of how people communicate with one another through various mediums, such as language, media, and technology.\n\nThese are just a few examples of the types of classes that might be considered to be in the humanities. There are numerous other subjects that could also be included in this category.
