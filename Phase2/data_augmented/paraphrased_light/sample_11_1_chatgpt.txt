what does the president of the usa do The President of the United States is the head of the executive branch of the federal government and is the commander-in-chief of the United States Armed Forces. The President is responsible for enforcing federal laws and overseeing the administration of the government. The President additionally has the power to negotiate treaties with foreign countries and to nominate federal judges and other officials, subject to confirmation by the Senate. The President is responsible for representing the United States on the world stage and for setting the overall direction of the country. The President also has the power to propose and veto legislation, and to grant pardons for federal offenses.
