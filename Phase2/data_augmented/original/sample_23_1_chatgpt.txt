what is customary at shiva

Shiva is a Hindu tradition that is observed following the death of a loved one. It is a period of mourning in which the family and friends of the deceased gather together to offer their condolences and support to one another.\n\nDuring the shiva period, it is customary for the family of the deceased to receive visitors in their home. Visitors may bring food or other gifts to offer their condolences and support. It is also common for people to recite prayers and say special psalms or verses from religious texts.\n\nIn some traditions, it is customary for the family of the deceased to sit on low stools or benches during the shiva period, as a sign of their mourning and humility. It is also common for the family to cover mirrors in the home, as a sign of respect for the deceased.\n\nThe length of the shiva period varies, but it typically lasts for seven days. At the end of the shiva period, the family may hold a special ceremony to mark the end of the mourning period and to honor the memory of the deceased.
